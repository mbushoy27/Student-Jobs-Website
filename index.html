Inspiration

I used Uber.com and Squareup.com as inspiration for my long scrolling, Z-pattern webpage. I really like the way that Square's website looks, with small bits of information in each section. No part of the website seems too busy while scrolling down the page, which I tried to emulate in my own webpage. I also liked the utilization of the z-pattern in the nav bar and throughout the page. Similar to Square, I used a simple and horizontally linear nav bar, and kept my home "page" simple. Uber also utilizes a similar use of the Z-pattern, but I especially liked the typographic styles used on Uber's page. The fonts used on Ubers webpage are simple, and the typography is spaced out so that the text is especially readable. I used inspiration from Uber's simple, but readable, typography by finding similar fonts and working with the line-spacing.

Template

I used Frame as a long-scrolling template for this website. Frame uses the Bootstrap Framework. From this template, I utilized the animations, and bits and pieces from the nav bar, header and footer.

Fonts

For my website, I used Alegreya, Maven Pro, and Roboto Condensed. I used Google Fonts to find all three fonts. As my site focuses on student jobs (similar to a portfolio), I tried to keep the fonts professional and simple. I used Alegreya as the font for my main header on the home page. This font sticks out the most out of the three, but still pairs well with Maven Pro and Roboto Condensed. Using Alegreya for the main header, I hope the draw the viewer in, while still maintianing professionalism. Throughout the website, I use Maven Pro for headers and Roboto Condensed for paragraphs and blocks of text. Both Maven Pro and Roboto Condensed are simple and speak to the professionalism of the website. Maven Pro is larger and has more line spacing, so it serves as a header. Roboto Condensed, on the other hand, has a similar format, but is smaller and easier to read for larger chunks of text.
